# Sprint-Review-and-Retrospective-SNHU-Travel-Enhancement-Project
Agile Software Development Lifecycle project for SNHU Travel

## Professional Reflection: Software Development & Agile

### Interpreting User Needs
I interpret user needs by treating **user stories** as "placeholders for a conversation" rather than static, rigid requirements. By prioritizing verbal collaboration and direct feedback—such as discovering that travelers value personalized recommendations over complex filters—I ensure that development efforts are focused on delivering the highest value to the end user.

### Development Approach & Agile Evolution
My approach to program development is rooted in **empiricism**: making decisions based on observed results and adapting as new information arises. This was particularly effective during mobile feature integration, where a flexible mindset allowed the team to pivot without restarting the planning process. In my future work, I plan to incorporate:
*   **Backlog Refinement:** To identify technical challenges early and establish a clear **Definition of Done**.
*   **Synchronized Daily Scrums:** Moving beyond "status reporting" to focus on true team synchronization and obstacle removal.

### The Role of a Team Member
To me, being a good team member means fostering **synergy** between all Scrum roles—Product Owners, Developers, and Testers. It involves active participation in defining **Acceptance Criteria** and maintaining transparent communication to reduce rework. I believe that a team’s success depends on how well we collaborate to manage the complexities of the product’s lifecycle.
